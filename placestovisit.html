India is a country rich in culture, history, and natural beauty, offering a vast array of destinations for every type of traveler. For history enthusiasts, the majestic Taj Mahal in Agra, the ancient ruins of Hampi, and the forts and palaces of Rajasthan, like the Amber Fort in Jaipur and the Mehrangarh Fort in Jodhpur, are must-see landmarks. Nature lovers will be captivated by the serene beauty of Kashmir's Dal Lake, the misty hills of Darjeeling, and the backwaters of Kerala. Spiritual seekers can visit the sacred cities of Varanasi and Rishikesh, or explore the temples of Khajuraho and the Golden Temple in Amritsar. Beach lovers can head to Goa for its lively coastline, or visit the quieter, more pristine beaches in Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Whether you're looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, India has a place that will captivate your soul.

India’s historical landmarks offer a deep dive into its rich past, from ancient civilizations and empires to colonial rule and independence. Whether you are interested in Mughal architecture, ancient temples, or colonial monuments, these sites offer a fascinating glimpse into the diverse history and culture of India. Visiting these historical places is not just about seeing beautiful structures but understanding the stories and struggles that shaped India into the vibrant nation it is today.

From ancient temples and forts to colonial-era buildings, the country's historical landmarks offer a fascinating journey through time. Here are some of the most iconic historical places to visit in India:

1. Taj Mahal, Agra

Visit the Taj Mahal, the quintessential symbol of love, whose magnificence is unparalleled in history and is now one of Delhi’s weekend trips. Shah Jahan commanded the construction of this lavish white marble building for his deceased wife, Mumtaz Mahal, in 1632. It took approximately 22 years to create this majestic monument, considered one of India’s most famous historical monuments.According to local legend, it was believed that Shah Jahan cut off the hands of all the workers who built the Taj Mahal so that a similar monument could not be built.

  • Entry Fee: Indians- INR 40, Foreigners- INR 1000 Onwards, No entry below 15 years
  • Timings: 30 Minutes before sunrise and closes 30 Minutes before sunset
  • Best Time To Visit: February-April
  • Special Attractions: Night Shows
  • Interesting Fact: A shopping complex and hotel inspired by the Taj Mahal is being constructed in Dubai and is estimated to be four times larger in size.

2. Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

The ‘Palace of Winds’ or Hawa Mahal gets its name due to the fact that it looks like a honeycomb of a beehive with its 953 intricate windows. It is also shaped like a crown as the ruler who built it, Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, was a major devotee of Lord Krishna. This magnificent attraction is one of the popular tourist places in Jaipur and is among the India’s famous buildings due to its curved architecture. It is also known as the tallest building in the world that doesn’t have any foundation. The palace is curved but still stands firm due to its pyramidal shape.

It has become a shooting spot for many national and international films.It is a five-storied building that is 15 meters high from its base. There is also a courtyard in front of the lower two stories while the width of the upper three stories is equal to that of a room. There are 953 windows in this palace, out of which air flows the palace.. Coming from the side of the City Palace, we can enter the Hawa Mahal through the royal gate which opens into a huge courtyard.

  • Entry Fee: Indians- INR 10, Foreigners- INR 50 Onwards
  • Timings: 9.30 am to 4.30 pm
  • Best Time To Visit: November-February
  • Nearest Airport: Jaipur Airport
  • Interesting Fact: It was believed that this building was created so that the royal ladies could look outside as they were constantly under ‘purdah’

3. Golden Temple, Amritsar

One of the most famous Gurudwara in the entire world, Sri Harmandir Sahib is one of the important spiritual places in India. While it has been around for a long while, it was rebuilt with pure gold and marble in 1830 by Maharaja Ranjit Singh. Located in the heart of Amritsar, it is visited by more than a hundred thousand pilgrims every day. It is considered one of the top five historical monuments of India and a must-visit attraction for those who are seeking the best of all in India.

  • Ticket Price: No Charge, Open for all.
  • Timings: Changes every month
  • Special Attractions: Amrit Sarovar
  • Best Time To Visit: October to March
  • Interesting Fact: Each day, over a hundred thousand people partake in the Langar here

4. Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata is one of the most historical tourist places in India. It was built during the peak of the British era. The then Viceroy Lord Curzon laid down the idea of this monument but its actual design was done by Sir William Emerson.

Multitudes of lush gardens, a museum filled with British memorabilia including weapons, paintings, sculptures, artefacts, etc and a royal portrait of the Queen are what you will find in this beautiful monument. One of the best paintings you will find here is the one by Russian artist Vasili Verestchagin. This painting depicts the Prince of Wales in Jaipur in 1876.

  • Entry Fee: Indians- INR 30, Foreigners- INR 200 Onwards
  • Timings: 5:30 am to 6:15 pm daily
  • Special Attractions: Black Statue of Angel of Victory
  • Best Time To Visit: November-February
  • Interesting Fact: There are two sets of mysterious inscriptions on the monument. ‘VRI’ and the other is ‘Dieu Et Mon Droit’. The first means Victoria Queen and Empress and the latter means ‘God and my right’.

5. Red Fort, Delhi

One of the historical tourist places in India, the Red Fort was constructed over ten years from 1638 to 1648. This fort was constructed when Shah Jahan shifted the capital from Agra to Delhi and it was then known as the Qila-e-Mubarak. This octagonal fort is one of the most famous historical places in north India and is also the site where the President gives his speech on Independence Day. The Red fort is truly remarkable and one of the best historical places in India.

  • Entry Fee: Indians- INR 10, Foreigners- INR 250 Onwards
  • Timings: 9:30 am to 4:30 pm daily (Closed on Mondays)
  • Special Attractions: Rang Mahal (The Palace of Colors)
  • Nearest Airport: Indira Gandhi International Airport
  • Interesting Fact: The Red fort was actually white as it was made of limestone. The British painted it red when the limestone started chipping off.

6. Meenakshi Amman Temple, Madurai


This gorgeous Hindu temple on the bank of the River Vaigai in Madurai focuses on seeking pleasant energies, positivism, and pure devotion. It is devoted to Parvati, also recognized as Meenakshi, & Shiva, her husband.. It is dedicated to the goddess Meenakshi, a form of Parvati, her consort Sundareśvarar, a form of Shiva and her brother Aḻagar, a form of Vishnu. The temple is theologically significant as it represents a confluence of the Shaivism, Shaktism and Vaishnavism denominations of Hinduism.

Appreciate the temple’s tremendous grandeur and marvel at its majestic construction with elaborate sculptures. It is one of India’s most well-known tourist destinations.

  • Entry Fee: No Entry fee
  • Timings: 9 am to 7 pm
  • Constructed By: Raja Kulasekara Pandya
  • Special Attractions: Pillars and 14 Gateways
  • Interesting Fact: The intricate architecture of the place is amongst the seven wonders of the world.

7. Amer Fort, Jaipur

Only 11 km from the Pink City Jaipur, Amer Fort is one of the most magnificent forts in India. Built by Maharaja Man Singh in 1592, it was truly the residence of kings. Another UNESCO site in the heart of Rajasthan. Made of yellow and pink sandstone, it is an unforgettable sight. Over 5 thousand people visit this fort daily, making it one of the most visited places in Jaipur.

  • Entry Fee: Indian Adults INR 25, Indian Students INR 10, Foreigners INR 200
  • Timings: 9 am to 6 pm
  • Best Time To Visit: October-December
  • Special Attractions: Interior Designs and Ride of Elephant
  • Interesting Fact: King Man Singh I was one of the most decorated generals of King Akbar

There are many more places to explore :

Munnar

Located in : Kerala

Famous for the tea estates, greenery, winding roads, blanket of mist, and viewpoints, Munnar is a hill station in Kerala, located in the Idukki district. Lying in the Western Ghats at 1600 metres, it is one of the most sought after and visited travel destinations globally.

Gulmarg

Located in : Jammu and Kashmir

Situated at an altitude of 2730 m above sea level, Gulmarg is a popular skiing destination located in Pir Panjal Range of Union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. Surrounded by snow-covered lofty Himalayas, meadows of flowers, deep ravines, evergreen forested valleys.

Darjeeling

Located in : West Bengal

Darjeeling, the former summer capital of India under the British Raj, has evolved into one of India's most sought-after hill stations. This picturesque hill destination in West Bengal is ideal for a romantic honeymoon. Darjeeling, nestled among acres of tea estates, is 2,050 metres above sea level.

Rishikesh

Located in : Uttarakhand

Located in the foothills of the Himalayas along the convergence of Ganga and Chandrabhaga River, Rishikesh is a small town in the Dehradun district, located close to Haridwar in Uttarakhand. Rishikesh (also called as Hrishikesh) is known for its adventure activities, ancient temples.

Nainital

Located in : Uttarakhand

Nainital, a picturesque hill station in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, is known for its serene lakes, lush green hills, and pleasant climate. Located approximately 300 kilometers from Delhi, Nainital town is centered around the emerald-green Naini Lake, which offers boating opportunities.

Goa

Located in : Goa

Lying on the western coast, Goa is India's smallest state and unlike any other, known for its endless beaches, stellar nightlife, eclectic seafood, world-heritage listed architecture. Spread across just 3,702 km, Goa lies in the Konkan region. It is a far cry from the hippie haven.
