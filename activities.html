Shopping

India is one of the best Asian countries to go shopping. The fact that their prices are very low compared to those in the UK and the wide variety of products make it impossible to come home from this country without any souvenirs.

Some of the most famous products are silk, silver jewelry and Indian tea. Jewels and precious stones are other desirable items purchased by tourists on their trips to Jaipur, where gems, gold, and diamonds and abundant in many of the markets. If you are hoping to buy carpets, the best can be found in Kashmir, where it is possible to find authentic silk carpets, and also in Darjeeling and Bhadohi.

Festivals

India during its festive season, particularly from October to December, offers a vibrant and immersive experience, with festivals like Diwali, Christmas, and others, bringing the country to life with celebrations, cultural events, and unique traditions.

Visiting India or travelling India is much more fun. Here's a glimpse of what you can expect:

  • Diwali (October-November) : This festival of lights is celebrated with fireworks, colorful decorations, and the exchange of sweets and gifts.
  • Christmas (December): While not as widely celebrated as Diwali, Christmas is a major festival, especially in certain regions, with churches decorated and special events taking place.
  • Other Festivals: Depending on the region, you might encounter other festivals like Onam (harvest festival in Kerala), Hornbill Festival (Nagaland), or Chhath Puja (Bihar).
  • Cultural Experiences: Festive seasons offer a unique opportunity to witness and participate in local traditions, rituals, and customs.
  • Festive Season Retail: The festive season is a busy time for retail, with consumers planning to spend more each year, thus boosting the economy.

There are many other things which can be done by people of different interests. Some of these are :

For the Adventurer:

  • Trekking in the Himalayas: Explore regions like Kasol, Kheerganga, Triund, or Annapurna, focusing on sightseeing, culture, and homestays.
  • Wildlife Safaris: Go on safaris in Corbett National Park to observe wildlife like tigers, elephants, and various bird species.
  • Camel Safari in Jaisalmer: Experience the Thar Desert and its unique landscape on a camel safari.

For the History Buff:

  • Discover Jungles and Palaces in Rajasthan: Explore forts, palaces, and wildlife in Rajasthan, including staying in jungle camps.
  • Step into History at Hampi: Explore the ancient ruins and temples of Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

For the Relaxing Traveler:

  • Goa: Enjoy beach holidays in Goa, known for its warm climate and activities.
  • Kerala Backwater Houseboat: Cruise through the scenic backwaters of Kerala in a traditional houseboat.
  • Relax in Homestays: Experience local culture and hospitality by staying in homestays in the Himalayas or other regions.
